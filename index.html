About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at MIT, advised by Prof. Vivek Farias (MIT ORC) and also mentored by Prof. Andrew Li at CMU. I earned my Bachelor's in Computer Science from Tsinghua University (Yao Class). 

I am broadly interested in developing machine learning and causal inference algorithms for large-scale dynamic decision-making systems in the real world. In particular, I focus on developing next-generation Experimentation Platforms, which provide scalable, low-cost solutions for discovering beneficial strategies/policies. I’ve had the pleasure of collaborating with Anheuser-Busch InBev (ABI), Takeda,Tiktok, Liberty Mutual, and the Broad Institute. My work for building a pioneering experimentation platform with Anheuser-Busch InBev won the Daniel H. Wagner Prize in 2022 (INFORMS news). In addition, some of my work has been recognized as two NeurIPS oral presentations, winner for the APS Best Student Paper Prize and RMP Jeff McGill Student Paper Award, and finalist for the MSOM Best Student Paper Prize.

Update: I will join Columbia Business School as an assistant professor in the Decision, Risk, and Operations division, starting Summer 2024.
