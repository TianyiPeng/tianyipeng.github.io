About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at MIT, luckily advised by Prof. Vivek Farias at MIT Operations Research Center (MIT ORC) and also mentored by Prof. Andrew Li at CMU.

I am broadly interested in, both theoretically and practically, developing machine learning algorithms for large-scale dynamic decision-making systems in real world.

My current reseach directions are Causal Machine Learning: an emerging field that aims to integrate causal inference into machine learning algorithms to improve the robustness, interpretablity, and trustworthiness of AI, which marries different fields such as econometrics, computer science, and operations research.

I love to collaborate with industry partners and have been working on empirtical applications ranging from operations management, bioinformatics, to recommendation systems.
