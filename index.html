About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at MIT, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Vivek Farias at MIT Operations Research Center (MIT ORC) and also mentored by Prof. Andrew Li at CMU.

I received a Bachelor's degree in computer science from Yao Class (exceptional pilot CS program led by Prof. Andrew C. Yao) in 2017.

I am in the job market 2022-2023.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in developing machine learning algorithms for large-scale dynamic decision-making systems in the real world.

My current research directions are Machine Learning and Causal Inference, an emerging field that marries different areas such as operations research, statistics, computer science, and econometrics.

In particular, I focus on developing the next-generation Experimentation Platform, which aims to provide scalable and low-cost solutions for identifying beneficial strategies/policies that otherwise are impossible to be discovered. Such a platform uses statistics, machine learning, and reinforcement learning together with robust validations to overcome real-world causal inference challenges.

Along this line, I collaborated with Anheuser-Busch InBev (ABI), the world's largest beer producer, to develope their physical retailer experimentation platform. I am also working with Takeda, a giant pharmaceutical company, to tackle the late and mis-diagnosis issues of patients through causal inference.

My other industry partners include Tiktok, Liberty Mutual, and Broad Institute, where I am developing practical machine learning solutions through a causal lens.

Some of the work have been selected as finalists in APS Student Paper Competition and Wagner Prize.
