About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at MIT, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Vivek Farias at MIT Operations Research Center (MIT ORC) and also mentored by Prof. Andrew Li at CMU.

I am broadly interested in, both theoretically and practically, developing machine learning algorithms for large-scale dynamic decision-making systems in the real world. My current research directions are Machine Learning and Causal Inference, an emerging field that marries different areas such as econometrics, computer science, and operations research.

In particular, I focus on developing the next generation Experimentation Platform, which aims to provide scalable and low-cost solutions for identifying beneficial strategies/policies that otherwise are impossible to be discovered. Such a platform uses machine learning, bandit, and reinforcement learning algorithms to overcome real-world causal inference challenges such as

  • the environment is highly noisy and non-stationary

  • the groups have complicated interactions and interference

  • the results suffer from the adherence and compliance issues

Along this line, I collaborated with Anheuser-Busch InBev (ABI), the world's largest beer producer, to develope their physical retailer experimentation platform. I also worked with other industry partners including Tiktok, Liberty Mutual, Broad Institute, and Takeda, to develope practical machine learning solutions through a causal lens.

I am in the job market 2022-2023.
